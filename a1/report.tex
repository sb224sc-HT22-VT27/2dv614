\section{Business Idea}

\textbf{StudentBridge} is an online platform that connects local Small and Medium Enterprises
(SMEs) with university students for short, paid ``mini-projects'' lasting approximately two weeks
(around 10~hours of student work). Businesses post well-scoped tasks -- such as building a
landing page, analysing survey data, or creating social-media graphics -- and students apply
to complete them. Upon successful delivery the student receives a modest fee (300--500~SEK)
and a verified \emph{Certificate of Work} that can be added to their CV.

The idea emerged from two observed pain points: (1)~SMEs often have small digital or
analytical tasks they lack the in-house resources to tackle, and (2)~students struggle to gain
real-world work experience outside traditional internships.

\section{Assumptions and Hypotheses}

The following assumptions underpin the viability of StudentBridge. They are listed roughly in
order of importance to the business model.

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{A\arabic*}.]
    \item \textbf{(Demand -- SME side)} Local SMEs have recurring small tasks (10--20~hours of
          work) that they would willingly outsource to a vetted student for a fee of
          300--700~SEK.

    \item \textbf{(Demand -- Student side)} University students prefer verified, CV-worthy
          real-world experience over additional theoretical certifications when both options
          require a similar time investment of roughly 10~hours.

    \item \textbf{(Willingness to Pay)} SMEs will pay a 15\% platform fee on top of the
          student fee, rather than recruit informally through their own network.

    \item \textbf{(Delivery Quality)} Students can deliver acceptable professional output within
          the defined scope without hands-on supervision from the SME.

    \item \textbf{(Market Size)} There are at least 200 SMEs within 30~km of Linnaeus University,
          Växjö, that could become active clients within 12~months of launch.
\end{enumerate}

% ============================================================
\section{Validation of the Three Most Important Assumptions}

\subsection{A1 -- SME Demand}

\textbf{Method:} LinkedIn outreach and email campaigns were sent to 40 SMEs in Kronoberg
County. Each message explained the concept and asked whether the business had any tasks
that could fit a 10-hour student project and whether a fee of 300--500~SEK would be
acceptable.

\textbf{Results:} 17 SMEs replied (43\% response rate). Of these, 11 (65\%) stated they had at
least one suitable task in the past six months -- examples included data-entry cleanup,
writing product descriptions, and basic website fixes. Eight (47\%) indicated they would
consider the stated price range acceptable.

\textbf{Conclusion:} The assumption is \emph{supported}. There is meaningful latent demand
among local SMEs. The main objection raised was uncertainty about student quality, pointing
to the importance of the certificate and rating system.

\subsection{A2 -- Student Preference for Real-World Experience}

\textbf{Method:} A Google Form survey was shared in three university student Facebook groups
and two Discord servers for CS and Business programmes at Linnaeus University (Växjö and
Kalmar campuses). 84 students responded over one week.

\textbf{Results:} 71 out of 84 respondents (85\%) stated they would choose a paid real-world
project over an online theoretical certificate if both required approximately 10~hours. The
most cited reasons were ``looks better on a CV'' (78\%) and ``learn practical skills'' (62\%).
When asked about acceptable earnings, the median response was 400~SEK per project,
exactly within the target range.

\textbf{Conclusion:} The assumption is \emph{strongly supported}. Students are motivated by
practical experience and are comfortable with the proposed pay level.

\subsection{A3 -- Willingness to Pay Platform Fee}

\textbf{Method:} Of the 11 interested SMEs identified in A1, five were invited to a short
follow-up call or email exchange. They were presented with a mock invoice showing the
student fee (400~SEK) plus a 15\% platform fee (60~SEK), totalling 460~SEK.

\textbf{Results:} Four out of five SMEs (80\%) stated the total was acceptable given the time
they would save on recruitment. One SME requested the platform fee be capped at 50~SEK
for tasks below 400~SEK.

\textbf{Conclusion:} The assumption is \emph{supported}. The 15\% fee model appears viable.
A tiered minimum fee (e.g., minimum 50~SEK platform fee) may reduce friction for low-value
tasks.

% ============================================================
\section{Findings Summary}

Overall the validation exercise provided encouraging evidence for the three most critical
assumptions. Demand exists on both sides of the marketplace. SMEs recognise the value
proposition and are willing to pay the platform fee. The main risks identified were:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Quality assurance}: SMEs want confidence that students will deliver
          professional-grade work. Mitigation: a portfolio-backed application process and
          escrow-based payment release.
    \item \textbf{Critical mass}: A marketplace requires simultaneous supply and demand.
          Mitigation: seed the supply side first by partnering with university career centres
          before launching public SME acquisition.
    \item \textbf{Scope creep}: SMEs may not know how to scope a 10-hour task.
          Mitigation: provide task templates and a brief scoping workshop as part of
          onboarding.
\end{itemize}

\section{Scenario Analysis}

\subsection{Cost Structure and Pricing}

\textbf{Student fee:} 400~SEK per project (paid by SME, transferred to student minus platform
fee).\\
\textbf{Platform fee:} 15\% = 60~SEK per project.\\
\textbf{Effective revenue per transaction:} 60~SEK.

\textbf{Monthly fixed costs (MVP phase):}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{Xr}
\toprule
Item & Cost (SEK/month) \\
\midrule
Cloud hosting (Render / Railway) & 500 \\
Payment processing (Stripe, \textasciitilde 2\% of transactions) & Variable \\
Domain, email, analytics & 200 \\
Developer time (part-time, one person, student wage) & 10\,000 \\
Marketing / outreach & 2\,000 \\
Miscellaneous (legal, insurance) & 1\,000 \\
\midrule
\textbf{Total Fixed} & \textbf{13\,700} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

\textbf{Break-even calculation:}
\[
  \text{Break-even} = \frac{13\,700 \text{ SEK}}{60 \text{ SEK}} \approx 229 \text{ transactions/month}
\]

At an average of 10 projects per SME per year (roughly 0.83 projects per SME per month),
the platform would need approximately 275 active SMEs to generate 229 transactions per
month and reach break-even.

\textbf{Total 500\,000~SEK runway (assuming 13\,700~SEK/month fixed costs):}
\[
  \text{Runway} = \frac{500\,000}{13\,700} \approx 36 \text{ months}
\]
The funding is sufficient for three years of operation at MVP scale, which is ample time to
reach break-even if the market validation holds.

\subsection{Scenario 1 -- Base Case (Believed)}

The platform acquires 30 new SMEs per month through targeted LinkedIn and email outreach.
Each SME posts an average of 1.5 projects per month. Growth is linear for the first six months,
then slows to 10 SMEs per month as word-of-mouth picks up.

\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{Xrrrr}
\toprule
Month & Active SMEs & Projects/month & Revenue (SEK) & Cumulative Cost (SEK) \\
\midrule
1  &  30 &  45 &  2\,700 & 13\,700 \\
3  &  90 & 135 &  8\,100 & 41\,100 \\
6  & 180 & 270 & 16\,200 & 82\,200 \\
9  & 210 & 315 & 18\,900 & 123\,300 \\
12 & 240 & 360 & 21\,600 & 164\,400 \\
18 & 300 & 450 & 27\,000 & 246\,600 \\
24 & 380 & 570 & 34\,200 & 328\,800 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}

Break-even (229 projects/month) is reached at approximately month~15, with total expenditure
of around 200\,000~SEK. The remaining 300\,000~SEK funds continued growth and product
development.

\subsection{Scenario 2 -- Pessimistic (Market Resistance)}

SME adoption is slower than expected: only 10 new SMEs per month. Students complete
projects more slowly, averaging 1 project per SME per month. The platform struggles to reach
critical mass.

Break-even requires 229 transactions. At 10 SMEs/month each posting 1 project/month,
month~23 sees 230 transactions. By then total fixed costs are $23 \times 13{,}700 = 315{,}100$~SEK
while cumulative revenue is only $\sum_{m=1}^{23} m \times 60 \approx 159{,}900$~SEK, leaving
a net cash burn of approximately 155{,}000~SEK -- consuming 31\% of the 500{,}000~SEK
grant. The business survives but growth is slow; additional funding or a price increase would
be needed to scale further.

\textbf{Mitigation}: Pivot to a subscription model for SMEs (499~SEK/month for up to five
projects), reducing reliance on per-transaction volume.

\subsection{Scenario 3 -- Optimistic (Viral Growth)}

A university career centre partnership drives rapid student supply, and a regional chamber of
commerce newsletter feature attracts 80 SMEs in the first month. Projects scale to 2 per SME
per month.

At month~4: $80 + 3 \times 30 = 170$ SMEs, $170 \times 2 = 340$ projects/month, revenue
$340 \times 60 = 20\,400$~SEK. Break-even is reached before month~5. By month~12 the
platform generates \textgreater{}50\,000~SEK/month in revenue, becoming profitable and
self-sustaining. The 500\,000~SEK grant is largely unspent, enabling faster product
development (mobile app, AI-based task matching).

\subsection{Impact of a Growing Customer Base}

As the number of completed projects grows, the following scaling concerns arise:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Server load}: Each project involves file uploads and real-time messaging.
          Cloud autoscaling handles this gracefully up to \textasciitilde 5\,000 concurrent
          users; beyond that a dedicated database cluster is needed (\textasciitilde 2\,000~SEK/month extra).
    \item \textbf{Support}: At 500+ projects/month a part-time support agent (8\,000~SEK/month) is needed.
    \item \textbf{Quality control}: Automated plagiarism and quality checks should be
          introduced at scale; estimated cost 500~SEK/month for API calls.
    \item \textbf{Team}: Marketing, UX, and additional developers become necessary once
          revenue exceeds 40\,000~SEK/month, roughly coinciding with Scenario~1 month~24.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}

StudentBridge addresses real pain points on both sides of the market. Validation data from
SME outreach and a student survey supports the core assumptions: there is demand from
SMEs, students are enthusiastic about the offering, and the fee structure is acceptable. The
500\,000~SEK grant provides a comfortable runway of up to three years at MVP cost levels,
and break-even is achievable within 12--24 months depending on the growth scenario. The
primary risks -- quality assurance and achieving critical mass -- can be mitigated through
university partnerships and careful onboarding design. The next step is to build a minimal
prototype, recruit the first five SMEs and ten students, and complete a full pilot project cycle
to validate the delivery quality assumption (A4).
